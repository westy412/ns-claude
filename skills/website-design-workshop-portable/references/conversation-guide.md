# Conversation Guide

> **When to read:** At the start of a workshop session and when suggesting topics for the team member to discuss with the client.

This guide covers the topics to capture per brand and suggested questions to prompt discussion.

---

## Topics Checklist

For each brand, capture information on all of the following. Use this as a coverage tracker.

| # | Topic | Priority | Notes |
|---|-------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Brand overview & context | Must have | Who they are, what they do, target audience |
| 2 | Color scheme | Must have | Primary, secondary, accent colors. Existing brand colors? |
| 3 | Typography | Must have | Font preferences, heading vs body style |
| 4 | Copywriting style | Must have | Tone of voice, formal vs casual, jargon level |
| 5 | Page list & hierarchy | Must have | What pages, parent/child relationships, depth |
| 6 | Navigation structure | Must have | How pages link, menu style, footer links, CTAs |
| 7 | Layout preferences | Should have | Spacious vs dense, dark vs light, visual weight |
| 8 | Key sections per page | Should have | Hero, features, testimonials, pricing, contact, etc. |
| 9 | Imagery & visual style | Should have | Photography, illustrations, icons, aesthetic |
| 10 | Reference websites | Nice to have | Sites they admire, competitors to differentiate from |
| 11 | Functionality & features | Should have | Forms, booking, e-commerce, blog, integrations |
| 12 | Responsive / mobile | Should have | Mobile priorities, app-like behavior, touch interactions |
| 13 | Brand differentiation | Nice to have | What makes this brand different from siblings in the group |

---

## Suggested Opening

When starting a session, present these categories to the team member:

"Here are the key areas we need to capture for each brand. You don't need to cover them in order -- just have the conversation naturally and paste back what you get. I'll extract and organise everything."

Then list the topics from the checklist above.

---

## Deep-Dive Questions by Topic

Use these to suggest follow-up questions when a topic needs more depth.

### Brand Overview & Context
- Who is the target audience for this brand?
- What problem does this brand solve for its customers?
- How does this brand sit within the wider group?
- Are there existing brand guidelines or assets?

### Color Scheme
- Do you have existing brand colors (hex codes, Pantone)?
- What feelings should the color palette evoke?
- Are there colors to avoid (competitor associations, cultural concerns)?
- Should the palette feel warm, cool, neutral, vibrant, muted?
- How many accent colors are needed?

### Typography
- Do you have existing brand fonts or licenses?
- Serif, sans-serif, or a mix?
- Should headings feel bold/impactful or elegant/refined?
- Body text priority: readability or character?
- Any specific font references you like?

### Copywriting Style
- Formal or conversational tone?
- Technical jargon or plain language?
- Short punchy sentences or flowing narrative?
- First person ("we") or third person ("the company")?
- What personality should come through in the writing?

### Page List & Hierarchy
- What are the must-have pages?
- Are there sections that need sub-pages (e.g., Services -> individual service pages)?
- How deep should the site go (2 levels, 3 levels)?
- Is there a blog or news section?
- Any pages that need gated content or login?

### Navigation Structure
- Single top nav or mega menu?
- Should the nav be sticky/fixed?
- What goes in the footer?
- Are there important CTAs that need to be always visible?
- How should users flow through the site (intended journey)?

### Layout Preferences
- Spacious with lots of white space or information-dense?
- Dark mode, light mode, or both?
- Full-width sections or contained/centered?
- Heavy on visuals or text-forward?
- Any layout patterns you've seen and liked?

### Key Sections per Page
- What must appear on the homepage above the fold?
- Are testimonials or social proof important?
- Do you need a pricing section?
- Contact form, chat widget, or both?
- Any unique sections specific to your industry?

### Imagery & Visual Style
- Photography-heavy or illustration-based?
- Do you have existing photography/assets?
- Abstract/artistic or clean/corporate?
- Icon style: line icons, filled, custom illustrations?
- Should images feel candid or staged/professional?

### Reference Websites
- What 2-3 websites do you admire (any industry)?
- What specifically do you like about them?
- Any competitor sites to differentiate from?
- Any sites that represent what you DON'T want?

### Functionality & Features
- Does the site need any forms (contact, booking, application)?
- E-commerce requirements?
- Blog or content management needs?
- Third-party integrations (CRM, analytics, chat)?
- Multi-language requirements?

### Responsive / Mobile
- What percentage of your audience is mobile?
- Are there mobile-specific features needed?
- Should it feel like a web app on mobile?
- Any mobile interactions that are important (swipe, tap, etc.)?

---

## Coverage Gap Detection

After processing each input, check the per-brand discussion log against this checklist. Report:
- **Covered:** Topics with at least basic information captured
- **Partially covered:** Topics mentioned but needing more detail
- **Not yet discussed:** Topics with no information

Present gaps as suggestions: "For {brand}, we still need to cover: {topics}. Here are some questions that might help..."
